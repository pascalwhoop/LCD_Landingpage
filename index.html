Analyzing facebook and other sources, we know where people will be!

Through our sophisticated systems, we analyze facebook and many other sources on the web to create a forecast of when people will be where.

And the best part: We even know what kind of people you'll run into, once you follow our lead, so you filter or let us recommend places to you, that you might like

What can we do for you?

These days many people RSVP to dozens of events a year. Facebook is a popular platform to create both small and large events and businesses use it to predict the amount of visitors they get.

However, it's quite tedious to check all the clubs and bars websites to find out where to spend the next friday night. This is especially difficult once you're in a new city.

Sure there are apps like Yelp that rate different locations, but those ratings are always based on the past and are sometimes years old...

We show you, what will be going on where and at what time based on data from now!

How it works?

We have 3 main questions to answer: where, when and what (or who)!

Where?

Using the facebook Graph API, we can read all public events and their attendants. This data, combined with some meta-heuristics and historical movement data, we're able to predict where people will spend their time in the next couple of days.

When?

When will people be wherever they will be? We analyze future facebook events and correlate these with movement data collected from the previous weeks. This way we know when people usually show up at different places and can give you an accurate prediction! So you never show up too soon (or too late).

What?

This question is especially tricky. So we found a place, where there will be ~2000 people on a saturday night but who knows, if you will actually get along with them? To answer this question, we perform a full text analysis for every event we parse. We then perform distance calculations to around 20 data-based categories that we obtained from analyzing thousands of events already. This way, if you enter a tag like "jazz", we'll filter our results for you and only show you the events that match the criteria. The analysis is based on a custom word stem algorithm which helps us understand "what" an event is really about.
